The Risk adjustment process has four major components.
  •   Development of the base rates for the risk adjusted populations.
 
  •   Development of algebraic expressions that relate demographic and clinical characteristics of beneficiaries to their expected, prospective covered health care costs, relative to the cost of the average person in the populations. This measure of cost relative to the average cost is known as the individual case score. By definition, the average cost beneficiary will have a case score of 1.0, others with more or less costly conditions and demographic characteristics will have scores that are greater or less than 1.0.
 
  •   Compilation of case scores for each beneficiary for whom requisite data are available and establishment of criteria to assign case scores to those without claims and eligibility data.
 
  •   Calculation of an average case mix for each participating contractor.
